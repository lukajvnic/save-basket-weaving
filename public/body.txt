I am writing to call for the full reversal of the recent changes to OSAP, decreasing the maximum non-repayable grant portion from 85% to 25%. 

Shifting grants into loans will force students to take on significantly more debt to access an education. Students are already under intense financial strain, as rent and groceries continue to rise, and the youth unemployment crisis has made part-time, co-op, and post-graduation work harder to secure. For many students, OSAP grants make the difference between being able to continue their education or not. Funding for post-secondary education is a public investment in Ontario’s workforce, technology and innovation, healthcare system, and broader economy. Saddling young people with debt undermines that investment. 

Students and families across Ontario are paying close attention to how elected officials respond. I urge you to stand with students on this issue and publicly support reversing these cuts.
